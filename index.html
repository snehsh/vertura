The Founder

"Most institutions teach students what to think. I built The Pinnacle Minds to teach students how to think — and why that difference changes everything."

The Pinnacle Minds was built on a simple but uncomfortable observation: the education system hasn't fundamentally changed in over a century, but the world it's preparing students for has transformed completely. In 2026, 65% of children entering school today will work in jobs that don't yet exist — yet we're still preparing them for jobs that do.

TPM exists to correct that gap. Not by teaching more subjects, but by building the foundational capabilities that remain constant regardless of what the future looks like: the ability to think clearly, execute consistently, communicate precisely, and adapt faster than the environment changes.

This is not a coaching centre. It is a structured development system — with measurable milestones, a quarterly development report, and a curriculum designed for the critical 12–15 age window when habits and thinking patterns are still being formed. Built for students who are ready to be challenged, pushed, and built into something exceptional.

How It Works

The Method.
Learn. Apply. Prove.

Teaching Ideology

Every session proves capability

01
Learn by Doing
Activities & live exercises

Concepts are tested inside the session — not at home, not on paper. Knowledge is stress-tested the moment it is introduced. If a student cannot apply it under mild pressure, they don't know it yet.

02
Reflect & Apply
Worksheets, case studies & personal audits

Students don't study concepts in theory — they map every framework onto their own real life. Every mental model becomes personal. Every bias audit becomes a mirror.

03
Prove It
Real scenarios, group challenges & presentations

Skills are verified by what a student does when the problem is real and the room is watching. No hiding behind theory. No partial credit for knowing without doing.

Session Structure

Four steps. Every session.

01 — Learn
◎
Concept-Driven Depth

One powerful concept per session. Real-world examples, case studies, research evidence. No surface-level information, no motivational padding — only depth that builds a permanent mental model.

02 — Apply
◈
Immediate Implementation

In the same session, concepts are applied. Written exercises, decision frameworks, real scenarios, partner challenges. Knowledge that stays in the head is knowledge wasted.

03 — Build
◇
Portfolio Outcomes

Every session produces a tangible output that goes into the student's portfolio — worksheets, blueprints, recorded speeches, project plans. Evidence that can't be faked.

04 — Review
◉
Accountability & Feedback

Every 12 sessions, parents receive a full Development Report — not a grade, not a certificate. Six measured dimensions of growth. Specific mentor observations. Real data.

At Completion

What you leave with

01
Course Certification

A formal certificate attesting to specific, measurable competencies built across all five levels — with scored dimensions, not just a programme name.

02
Real Portfolio

A 5-level portfolio of worksheets, projects, speech recordings, leadership logs, and habit records. Evidence that any university or institution can evaluate.

03
420-Day Habit Record

A comprehensive record of daily discipline across the full 18-month programme. Evidence of character that no exam, interview, or exam score can produce.

Common Questions

Questions
Answered.

Before You Apply
Is 12 or 13 years old too young for this?
+
Not at all — 12 to 15 is the perfect age, not too young. Every neuroscientist who studies the brain agrees: the habits and thinking patterns formed between 12 and 16 become the defaults your child runs on for the rest of their life. If we wait until 17 or 18, we are not building habits — we are trying to break old ones. That is three times harder. The students who begin at 13 and complete by 15–16 are not doing what their peers are doing. They are two to three years ahead.
My child is already too busy with school and tuitions.
+
Most students who are overwhelmed by school are not overwhelmed by the content — they are overwhelmed because they have never been taught how to manage their time, how to focus deeply, or how to organise their effort. Level 2 (Self Mastery) addresses this directly. The habit systems and deep work protocols we teach have helped students manage their school workload better, not worse. Three hours on a Saturday morning is less time than most students spend on their phone on a Sunday.
My child isn't interested — I can't force them.
+
You're absolutely right that forcing never works — and we agree completely. What we suggest: let your child be part of the assessment conversation. Not the enrollment decision. Just the conversation. Let them meet us, see the environment, and understand what other students their age are doing here. In our experience, the moment a 13-year-old walks into a room where students their age are being taken seriously — not talked down to, not fed worksheets, but actually challenged — they want to be part of it.
How do I know it will actually work?
+
That's the right question — and we can answer it. Every 12 sessions, you receive a Quarterly Development Report with actual measured scores across six dimensions: cognitive growth, discipline consistency, communication development, leadership behaviour, project quality, and mentor observations. You will see the numbers move. You will also see the change at home before the report arrives — in how your child handles a disagreement, in whether they pick up a responsibility before being told, in how they talk about their own future.
Can we wait until they are older?
+
You can — and that is a valid choice. But consider this honestly: the skills we build at 13 are permanent. The same skills built at 17 are still valuable, but they now compete with years of existing habits that need to be replaced first. Every year you wait is not a neutral decision. It is a year in which your child's defaults are being set by whatever environment they happen to be in. The window doesn't close at 16 — but it does narrow.
When are classes? How much time is required?
+
Classes run every Saturday morning, 3 hours per session. The next cohort begins September 5, 2026. Beyond Saturday sessions, students maintain a daily habit (5–20 minutes) and complete short weekly reflections. The total weekly commitment is approximately 3.5–4 hours. We designed it to complement, not compete with, school and other commitments.
What is the admission process?
+
After you submit the application form, we schedule a 20-minute assessment conversation — with the student, not just the parent. We ask three things: what are you good at that your school report card has never measured, what do you want to be known for, and where do you feel most stuck right now. There is no test. We are assessing readiness and genuine motivation. If it's a good fit, we confirm admission and discuss the starting date and fee structure.
